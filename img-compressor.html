FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does compression reduce image quality?
It depends on your compression level. "Low" compression is nearly lossless — you'll barely notice a difference. "High" compression reduces quality more noticeably in exchange for much smaller files. Use "Medium" for the best balance.
Are my images uploaded to a server?
Never. All compression happens directly in your browser using JavaScript. Your images never leave your device, so they're completely private and secure.
What image formats are supported?
The compressor supports JPG/JPEG, PNG, and WEBP formats. For PNG files, compression works best by slightly reducing quality or switching to WEBP using our IMG Converter tool.
How much can I reduce file size?
Results vary by image, but typically you can reduce JPG file sizes by 40–80% with minimal quality loss at Medium setting. High compression can achieve up to 90% reduction.
Is there a file size or count limit?
There's no enforced limit — you can compress as many images as your browser can handle. Very large batches may slow down depending on your device's memory and processing power.
